# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and write a bio that reflects your academic and professional interests. Recruiters and professors may look you up.

2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself whether you would be comfortable with your family, professor, or a future employer seeing the post. If not, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Review and update your privacy settings on all platforms regularly. Limit who can see your personal information, location, and contact details.

4. **Give credit when sharing others' work** – Always tag or mention the original creator when sharing articles, photos, or ideas that are not your own.

5. **Engage positively and constructively** – Comment with purpose. Whether you agree or disagree, keep your tone respectful and fact-based.

6. **Report harmful content** – If you come across cyberbullying, fake news, or inappropriate content, use the platform's report feature rather than engaging or sharing.

7. **Separate personal and professional accounts** – Consider maintaining a LinkedIn for professional networking and keeping personal accounts private.

---

## DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal details publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID number, or daily schedule on any public platform.

2. **Don't engage with strangers who send unsolicited messages** – Be cautious of unknown accounts asking for favours, money, or personal information — even if they appear friendly.

3. **Don't post during conflicts or when emotional** – Reacting impulsively during an argument online often makes situations worse. Step back, reflect, and respond calmly or not at all.

4. **Don't spread unverified information** – Before sharing news or forwards (especially on WhatsApp), verify the source. Spreading misinformation can cause real harm.

5. **Don't use social media to vent about your institution, professors, or classmates** – Even private posts can be screenshotted and shared. Negative posts about your college can affect your reputation and relationships.

6. **Don't click on suspicious links** – Avoid links sent by unknown contacts or links that seem too good to be true (prize claims, free recharges, etc.). These are often phishing attempts.

7. **Don't share login credentials** – Never share your username and password for any platform with anyone, even a friend.
