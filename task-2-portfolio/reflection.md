# Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio

## Platforms Set Up

1. **GitHub** (github.com)
2. **LinkedIn** (linkedin.com)
3. **Kaggle** (kaggle.com)

## Reflection

Setting up these three platforms felt like building a foundation for my professional life as a student. Each one serves a very different purpose, and understanding that difference made the task feel meaningful rather than just a formality.

GitHub is essentially a platform where developers store, share, and collaborate on code. As a CSE student, this is going to be one of the most important platforms I use throughout my degree. I created a profile README that introduces me, mentions my branch and year, and states that I hope to learn full-stack development and understand how AI systems are built from scratch. Even at this early stage, having a GitHub profile makes me feel like I am part of the developer community.

LinkedIn is the professional networking platform used by employers, recruiters, and industry professionals worldwide. I filled in my Education section with my current degree, institution, and expected graduation year. Over the next four years, I plan to use LinkedIn to connect with seniors, follow companies I am interested in, and eventually apply for internships directly through the platform.

Kaggle is a data science and machine learning community where people compete in challenges, share datasets, and learn through free courses. Since I am in CSE, I expect to work with data and machine learning tools later in my degree, and having a Kaggle account now means I can start exploring that space early.

Over the next four years, I plan to keep all three profiles updated — adding projects to GitHub as I build them, growing my LinkedIn network, and working through beginner data challenges on Kaggle.
