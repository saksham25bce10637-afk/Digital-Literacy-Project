# Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing forms of cybercrime in India. With over 10 billion UPI transactions processed every month, fraudsters have developed increasingly clever methods to deceive ordinary users — particularly young people and first-time smartphone users who are new to digital payments.

---

## A Realistic Scenario

Rohan, a 19-year-old college student in Bhopal, listed his old laptop for sale on an online marketplace. Shortly after posting, he received a call from someone claiming to be an interested buyer. The caller said he was in the Indian Army and could not visit in person but would pay immediately via Google Pay.

The caller sent Rohan a QR code and told him to "scan it to receive the payment." Rohan scanned the code, entered his UPI PIN as prompted, and was surprised to find that instead of receiving money, Rs. 8,500 had been debited from his account. The caller had sent a collect/payment request disguised as a receive link. By the time Rohan realised what had happened, the fraudster had disconnected and blocked his number.

---

## How It Typically Happens

1. The fraudster contacts the victim through a marketplace, social media, or a fake customer service number.
2. They build trust by posing as a buyer, bank executive, or delivery agent.
3. They send a **collect request or fake payment link** and instruct the victim to "enter their PIN to receive money."
4. The victim unknowingly authorises a payment out of their account.
5. The money is immediately transferred and the fraudster disappears.

---

## Who Is Targeted

Students selling items online, people seeking refunds, job seekers promised advance pay, and anyone unfamiliar with how UPI works — particularly those who do not know that you never need to enter your PIN to *receive* money.

---

## Consequences

Financial loss (often between Rs. 2,000 and Rs. 50,000+), emotional distress, and in some cases, identity theft if banking credentials are also stolen. Recovery of funds is difficult once transferred.
