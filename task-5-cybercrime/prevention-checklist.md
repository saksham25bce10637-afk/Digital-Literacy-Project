# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students (India)

## General Online Safety

1. **Never share your OTP with anyone** – No bank, UPI app, or government agency will ever ask for your OTP over a call or message. If someone does, hang up immediately.

2. **Lock your UPI apps with a PIN or biometric** – If your phone is lost or stolen, a separate app lock ensures your payment apps cannot be opened without authentication.

3. **Do not click on links received via SMS or WhatsApp** – Fake links often mimic official bank or UPI app URLs (e.g., "sbi-refund.in" instead of "sbi.co.in"). Always open apps directly.

4. **Verify before you trust** – If someone calls claiming to be from your bank or a company, hang up and call the official customer care number yourself to verify.

5. **Keep your UPI transaction limit low** – Most UPI apps allow you to set daily transaction limits. Keeping this at a reasonable amount reduces potential loss if your account is compromised.

6. **Check app permissions regularly** – Avoid giving unnecessary permissions (microphone, contacts, camera) to payment or shopping apps. Malicious apps may misuse these.

## UPI and Financial Safety (Specific Tips)

7. **Remember: You NEVER need to enter your PIN to receive money** – This is the most important rule in UPI safety. If someone asks you to scan a QR code or enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is a scam. Stop immediately.

8. **Double-check the recipient's UPI ID before sending** – A single wrong character in a UPI ID can send money to the wrong person. Always verify the registered name shown before confirming.

## If You Are Victimised

- **Report immediately to your bank's helpline** to block the transaction or account.
- **File a complaint at:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** (available 24x7)
- Keep all evidence — screenshots of chats, transaction IDs, and call logs — before reporting.
