# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
Canva (canva.com)

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is titled **"Be Digitally Smart"** and is designed as a single-page visual resource for first-year students. It covers the following three topics:

1. **What Digital Literacy Is** – A short definition explaining that digital literacy means having the skills to find, evaluate, create, and share information using digital technologies responsibly.

2. **Useful Digital Tools for Students** – A quick visual guide to tools like Google Docs, Canva, GitHub, and Google Forms that students can use for academic work and collaboration.

3. **Safe Internet Practices** – Key tips such as using strong passwords, not sharing personal information publicly, recognising phishing links, and keeping software updated.

## Reflection

Making this infographic was an interesting experience. I used Canva because it has ready-made templates that made it easy to get started without any design background. The most challenging part was deciding what information to include since digital literacy is a broad topic — I had to narrow it down to what would be most useful for a fellow first-year student. I chose to focus on tools and safety because those are the two things students need to know from day one. One thing I found particularly interesting was learning that digital literacy is not just about knowing how to use apps, but also about using them safely and responsibly. If I were to do this again, I would add a section on email etiquette since that is something most students are not taught formally.
